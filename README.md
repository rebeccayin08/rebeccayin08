<p>Hi, I'm <strong>Rebecca.</strong></p>

<p>
I'm interested in geophysics, glaciology, and seismology, both in the field and through computational analysis of archival data.  
</p>

<p>
On <strong>Juneau Icefields</strong>, I conduct glacier science fieldwork covering glaciology, glacial geomorphology, periglacial ecology, and climate processes through <a href="https://www.juneauicefield.org/students" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>the Juneau Icefield Research Program</strong></a> as the youngest participant.
</p>

<p>
At <a href="https://pgsl.eas.gatech.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Georgia Tech Polar Geophysical Simulation Lab</strong></a>, I analyze Ross Ice Shelf radargrams using MATLAB and Python to track ice thickness, interpret archival airborne data and radar attenuation profiles.
</p>

<p>
Previously, as a student researcher at 
<a href="https://sdl.gis.harvard.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Harvard Spatial Data Lab</strong></a>,
I analyzed urban heat island effects on Tianjin subway stations using geospatial explainable AI (Geo XAI) and visual tools; built data-driven solutions for sustainable urban planning.
</p>

<p><strong>Highlighted Presentations: </strong></p>

<p>
  <a href="https://agu.confex.com/agu/agu24/meetingapp.cgi/Paper/1732435" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Radiometric Interpretation of Archival Airborne Radio-Echo Sounding in Ross Ice Shelf</strong></a>: <strong><em>AGU 2024</em></strong>
  <br />
  Developed a radiometric calibration framework for 1974–75 SPRI/NSF/TUD airborne radar film records of the Ross Ice Shelf, recovering decadal-baseline subsurface observations from analog Z-scope data.
</p>

<p>
  <a href="https://agu.confex.com/agu/agu25/meetingapp.cgi/Paper/1955486" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Unlocking 1970s Airborne Radar Echo Sounding Survey in Ross Ice Shelf, Antarctica</strong></a>: <strong><em>AGU 2025</em></strong>
  <br />
  Built A.S.T.R.A., a custom computer vision tool to extract and radiometrically calibrate surface and basal radar reflections from 35mm archival A-scope film collected during the 1974–75 SPRI/NSF/TUD campaign, producing the first spatially referenced dataset of historic RIS radar power for multi-decadal comparison with modern records.
</p>
